The video reviews iONEO's entry-level handheld console, Air Mini, priced at just ¥499, capable of running classic games like PS2, 3DS, and NGC titles. Unboxing reveals simple accessories like a manual and charging cable. The console features a 4.2-inch 1280×960 IPS screen with a maximum brightness of 500 nits, offered in three color options. Powered by the MediaTek Dimensity G90T processor, it delivers decent performance, with particularly strong GPU capabilities. The joystick uses Hall-effect technology, supports press functionality, and customizable lighting effects. The retro-style button layout and soft shoulder keys provide a comfortable gaming experience.

The Air Mini has a 4:3 screen ratio, ideal for classic games. The video demonstrates gameplay for titles like *God of War*, *Resident Evil 4*, and *The Wind Waker*. Most games run smoothly at 2x resolution with stable frame rates and power consumption between 6-7 watts. Additionally, the console includes active cooling and a 4500mAh battery, ensuring good battery life.

The teardown showcased a neat internal structure, featuring components like cooling fans and vibration motors, reflecting solid build quality. In summary, Air Mini is compact, portable, and delivers reasonable performance, making it a great choice for entry-level gamers. Its iOSpace system and Quick Access menu enhance usability, and overall, it offers a satisfying gaming experience.
