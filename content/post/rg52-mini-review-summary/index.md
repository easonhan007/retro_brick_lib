In this video, the presenter reviews the RG52 Mini handheld console, the first to feature the RK3562 chip. The device is available in military green, gray-white, and black, with a 5000mAh battery. It features conductive rubber buttons, ambient lighting, and various ports such as HDMI output, TF card slot, headphone jack, and a Type-C port. The screen resolution is 720x1280, with a full-view IPS screen, operating on Linux 1E4.7.

The presenter tests the console's performance with a variety of games, including those from NDS, N64, PS1, DC, and PSP platforms. NDS games run smoothly, N64 games maintain a stable 50 FPS, and PS1 games perform exceptionally well. Notably, the RG52 Mini excels in PSP game performance, running GT Racing smoothly at double resolution and handling more demanding games like God of War smoothly at single resolution. The presenter highlights that RG52 Mini outperforms other devices with similar chips in running PSP games.

Additionally, the console supports WiFi and vibration feedback, and is affordably priced, making it suitable for Linux enthusiasts. It also supports a universal mod patch, enhancing the gaming experience. Overall, RG52 Mini is considered a leading option among low-end Linux handheld consoles due to its high performance and reasonable pricing.
