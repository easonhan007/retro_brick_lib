The video provides an in-depth review of the GKD PIXEL 2 handheld console, focusing on its design, performance upgrades, and user experience. As an upgraded version of the GKD PIXEL 1, the PIXEL 2 retains the aluminum alloy body design but switches its buttons from metal to plastic. The device is slightly wider and features a screen resolution upgrade to 640x480, offering clearer visuals. The system interface has also been improved, adopting an Android-like design while keeping the original Linux front end.

In terms of performance, the PIXEL 2 is equipped with an RK3326S processor, supporting various classic game categories such as arcade, handheld, and console games. Demonstrations include running "PMP" games, fighting games, and classics like "The Legend of Sword and Fairy," all showing smooth performance. The button feedback has also been refined, eliminating the "clicky" sound present in the first generation. The upgraded screen delivers better color and viewing angle performance.

Additionally, the PIXEL 2 includes vibration feedback and music playback functionality, which allows it to double as an MP3 player via the 3.5mm headphone jack. While the device is slightly bulkier, it offers a significantly enhanced experience, making it a solid "cyber walnut" handheld console.
