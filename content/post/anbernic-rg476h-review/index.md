Ambonick's new handheld, the RG476H, officially launches today with a starting price of 998 RMB. It features a 4.7-inch, 120Hz high refresh rate screen with a resolution of 1280×960. The screen has minimal bezels and uses a sleek glass front. The design includes conductive rubber D-pad with support pillars, Hall-effect joysticks with press functionality, and front-and-back arranged linear trigger buttons, providing a comfortable grip. The device is equipped with a 5000mAh battery supporting 10W charging and weighs 299g, making it lighter by 60g compared to the metal-bodied RG477M.

In terms of gaming performance, the RG476H proves stable in tests. It runs games like *Naruto Shippuden* and *Hyrule Warriors* at 30-60 FPS, making it ideal for classic retro and mid-tier Android games. Its 4:3 screen ratio and 900P resolution enhance the experience for PS1, PS2, and other retro gaming titles. Additionally, the handheld supports cloud gaming and offers complimentary membership to selected services.

As for internal build quality, the device includes a 5000mAh battery, cooling fan, and heat dissipation plate, with well-organized cables and sturdy internal design. The screen performs well in low-light conditions with minimal backlight bleed. Overall, the RG476H is a cost-effective handheld device tailored for retro gaming and casual use, appealing to light to moderate gamers.
