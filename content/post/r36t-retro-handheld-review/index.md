The video reviews the R36T handheld console, a variant of the R36S model. Its standout feature is the CRT-style convex screen design. Although visually unique, the screen is made of plastic, is not fully laminated, and lacks high-quality construction. Powered by the RK3326 processor, the device supports classic game emulation such as PS1, FC, and PSP games but struggles with demanding games. Performance issues were noted, especially in games like "Dragon Ball."

The console's button design shows deficiencies. The D-pad lacks proper support, leading to input errors and poor usability in precision-demanding games like fighting titles. The joystick performs better but does not offer smooth operation overall. The screenâ€™s physical mask mimics CRT aesthetics but introduces visual obstructions with black borders, negatively impacting the viewing experience. The ergonomic back design improves grip slightly, though the speaker placement is unconventional.

Upon disassembly, it was revealed that the R36T shares the same motherboard as the R36S, with its screen being the only notable modification. The 3.5-inch screen is not fully laminated and appears to be a downgrade compared to the R36S. The design changes fail to enhance user experience significantly, making the R36S a more cost-effective choice.

In conclusion, the R36T attempts to deliver a retro CRT look but falls short in functionality and design quality. The video recommends opting for the more reliable and affordable R36S model. Viewers are encouraged to share their thoughts in the comments or live chat.
